Learn About FIRE NSBE Jr.

FIRE NSBE Jr is a chapter of the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) for students, grades 5-12, interested in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM).

We are advised mostly by working engineers who are also members of the NSBE Greenbelt Space Alumni Chapter. FIRE (Future Innovative Rising Engineers) was named in 2009 by the students in the chapter to reflect their future goals in the STEM fields. We meet 3-4 Saturdays a month during the school year at the CMIT High School in Laurel, MD.

Frequently Asked Questions

CMIT Academy High School of the Chesapeake Lighthouse Foundation (CLF) currently allows FIRE to meet at their location every Saturday from 10 am to 1 pm. The launch location will be announced the week before the scheduled launch. Launches will rotate between fields in Laurel, Clarksville, and Mt Airy, MD.

The FIRE Rocket Challenge received serval grants and sponsorships for the inaugural program launch! The generous support allows us to bring the experience to each participant for only $50. Participants who choose to undertake FAI or sport competition flying with NAR, for national and international competitions will incur additional costs depending on the categories chosen.

No experience is necessary to have fun!! The instruction will be differentiated depending on the skill level of the participants. You can come with no experience or be a current competition flyer. All are welcomed!

All registration handled is via the FIRE NSBE Jr. website. Use this link for the registration form to create your account. http://www.firensbejr.org/join-now.html

There is an Aerospace Careers module taught throughout the competition season. Participants will explore the various career paths that all lead to aerospace. Information regarding high school internships and college scholarships will also be shared.
